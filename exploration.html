Our data structure¶

Our final dataset is composed of 104505 rows and 20 columns, there is no missing values and most of the columns provide informations about lenders financial situation and profile at the moment of the data recording.

Main feature of interest¶

The main feature of interest in the dataset is LP_CollectionFees, it represents how much it costs Prosper Loan to collect payments from borrowers. Higher is the collection fees, lower is the account value in termn of profitability to the company. Therefore, we will look at what are the characteristics of a bad borrower i.e. a borrower that is less/not profitable for the company.

Features supporting our investigation¶

It is not clear yet what may be all the features that could help support our investigation. We start be using common sense and intuition to explore a set features and eventually add some more if necessary. So, the features we will be starting with are the following: 'EmploymentStatus', 'Occupation', 'BorrowerState', 'CreditScore', 'DebtToIncomeRatio', because we are hypothesizing that those variables could be determinant on the ability of an applicant to qualify for a loan, get a competitive rate and be able to repay it back properly. CreditScore is not a feature that currently exist in our dataset, however we will be able to explore it by creating a new variable of the same name that will be equal to the average of CreditScoreRangeLower and CreditScoreRangeUpper. Before going any further, let's create it.

In [21]:
df['CreditScore'] = (df['CreditScoreRangeLower'] + df['CreditScoreRangeUpper'])/2

Conclusion¶

From the two last visualizations, the take away is is that the distribution of the observation doesn't change whether we look at different third variable like EmploymentStatus and CreditScore, with Occupation and LP_CollectionFees on the axes. We noticed that overall, borrowers do have below average credit score prior to qualify for a loan. Therefore what we conclude is that bad borrower behaviours seem to be related to a historic of wrong management of bill/debt payment even with sufficiently good financial standing to afford those pyment. We also have so isolated cases of borrowers with above average credit score who fell in the category of high collection fees, the justification is that they have started having this bad behaviour after they got the loan but this still confirm that those collection fees are by the fact that the company has hard time getting the money back from the borrowers because they are just not making their monthly payments even though they could definitely afford them.
